Appearance

Lion rex rabbits typically have short fur on thier bodies. Their tell-tale charasticic is the long fur thats around their neck and head. This fur resembles a lions mane hence the name "Lion Rex."

Lion Rex rabbits can come in a varity of colors. These colors can include, but aren't limited to:

  • Black
  • White
  • Grey
  • Tan
  • Bi-color

Lion Rex rabbits are typically on the smaller side for a domestic rabbit. They usually weigh in from about 2.5lbs to 3.75lbs. They usually measure from only 8 to 10 inches long.

Grooming

Lion Rex's have what is a called a double maned coat. Which is the mane that is around their neck and can also be on their rear end.

Due to the nature of the Lion Rex's fur, it is important to practice regular grooming. They also require more frequent grooming because of their manes.

Brushing them weekly can help keep the fur soft and prevent any mats or tangles. Always brush in the direction that the fur grows in.

Using a comb on the mane can be more effect and help break up any early mats or tangles that may appear in the mane.If the mane begins getting unruly trimming it can make your Lion Rex much more comfortable.

Regualr nail trimming is also important to maintain a happy and healthy rabbit. If trimming their nails isn't something you're comfortable with doing, seek out a rabbit vet or groomer in your area.

Rabbits are generally very clean animals and should rarely require human intervention in regards to bathing.

Temperament

Lion Rex rabbits are usually very friendly, playful and social. They can sometimes be very timid as well.

They are also very intelligent and can quickly learn potty training concepts.

Lion Rex Rabbits can also be taught commands such as play or come.

Caring for Your Lion Rex

Caring for your Lion Rex can come off as a daunting task. In reality, caring for any rabbit would fall in between the care required for cats and dogs. Meaning, they aren't as independant as a cat might be, but they also aren't as needy as a dog may be.

Some requirements for caring for your Lion Rex include:

  • A diet consisting of about 80% hay.The other 20% should be a combination of leafy greens such as romaine lettuce, and pellets.
  • Fruits or anything sweet should only be given as special treats.
  • Constant access to water is also important.
  • Access to a litterbox. Rabbits can be potty trained which makes clean up easier.
  • You can put a constant supply of hay in the literbox to promote and teach the habit of using it. Rabbits usually like to eat while they poop!
  • They should have ample room to play. You should not keep your rabbit locked in a cage 24/7. They need rooom to exercise and stretch those legs.
  • If you allow your rabbit to roam your house during play time, keep an eye out to make sure they arent nibbling anything they shouldn't be (ex. wires).
  • Providing them with enrichment can be a good way to promote healthy chewing and allow them to widdle their teeth.

Health Concerns

All animals have their own health concerns. Lion Rex rabbits are no different.Some common health concerns to look out for are:

  • Dental issues: Rabbits teeth grown constantly. It is important to supply them with enrichment and a balanced diet that can prevent their teeth from getting to long leading to infections and pain.
  • Fur Impactions:Due to their long fur, Lion Rex rabbits can injest fur while they are grooming themselves. Rabbits are unable to vomit which can lead to blockages and other complications. Regular brushing and a high fiber diet can help prevent this from becoming an issue.
  • Gastrointestinal Stasis:This happens when the digestive system slows or stops which can be life threatening. It is oftem caused by stress, lack of fiber, dehydration. Symptoms can include a lack of appetite, reduced bowel movements, or a bloated belly.
